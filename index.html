Nuclear Structure Physics Laboratory

Our laboratory is equipped with a variety of radiation detection and analytical facilities, including High-Purity Germanium (HPGe) detectors, X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF) set-up, LaBr₃(Ce) detectors, NaI(Tl) detector, and Geiger–Müller (GM) counter, which support research in nuclear physics, environmental radioactivity, and material characterization. The HPGe systems are routinely used for high-resolution gamma-ray spectroscopy, including studies of naturally occurring radionuclides such as 138La and 40K in ore-mimicking samples, agricultural products, spices, fertilizers, and biological tissues. The XRF facility is employed for both qualitative and quantitative elemental analysis of diverse materials, including archaeological excavation samples, jewellery, and food products. The LaBr₃(Ce) and NaI(Tl) detectors are utilized for timing measurements, gamma-ray spectroscopy, and environmental radiation surveys, while GM counters are used for radiation monitoring and educational purposes. Complementing these experimental facilities, the laboratory also has access to High-Performance Computing (HPC) resources at Shiv Nadar Institution of Eminence, enabling theoretical investigations such as large-scale shell-model calculations and other computational studies in nuclear structure physics.
